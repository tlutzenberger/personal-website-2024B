Hi, I'm Tom Lutzenberger. Welcome to my website and digital home. Here you'll find all things about me, what I do, what I can offer in skills and services, and general projects I'm tinkering with now and then.

I'm an avid reader and writer, and I'm regularly active online with blockchain projects and web developments at the grassroots level. I'm also formally trained in web design and network administration as well as work in online accessibility and cloud management. I've been a big pusher in my work world for data visualization and mining, as well as automation in government business processes.

I've been fortunate enough in life to have been able to take advantage of multiple educational opportunities, and I continue to be a lifelong learner. If I have the time, there's always something new to learn. My training is varied, cross-cutting, and applies both technical and soft skills. My portfolio includes:

  • Masters' in Business Administration, CSU Sacramento
  • BA in Anthropology, Arizona State University
  • BA in English, St. Mary's College
  • 10 associate degrees in Network Administration, Web Design, Database Management, Psychology, Fire Science, History and more.
  • 4 certificates in Geospatial Information Systems, IT, Digital Development and Photography.
Oh, and I dabble irregularly in Spanish and German at times too.

I'm currently at the crossroads starting a number of new projects. I've been a freelance writer for something near 19 years, and now I'm focusing more on web design packaging and digital content delivery as an evolution of my previous markets.

I'm also playing around with building websites that glean data from blockchains with APIs and JNode bridges, thereby making it easier for the average person to see trends in blockchain data versus having to know scripting commands to get to the same. Ideally, I want to build a set of functional tools for NFT holders with which to better understand the markets they trade in, as well as related trend movements. I'm primarily favoring Javascript and Python as my go-to tools for the related webpage prototypes.
